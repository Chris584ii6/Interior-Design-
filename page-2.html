Creating a balanced space

 

light plays a crucial role in achieving harmony in interior design. natural light can make a space feel open and airy, while artificial lighting can be used to highlight specific areas and create a cozy ambiance. by layering lighting sources and considering how they interact with textures and colours, you create an ambiance that feels seamless and inviting.

 

Curtains

 

understanding scale and proportion is essential for creating a balanced room. for example, using low curtains can make ceilings appear higher, and choosing the right size bed sheets can enhance the overall look of your bedroom.

 

TIP: follow the “two-thirds rule” for example, a coffee table should be about two-thirds the length of your sofa for a balanced look.

Mirrors can be used to create focal points and add depth to a room. Placing a mirror opposite a window can reflect light and make the space feel larger.

Tip: Arrange furniture and décor to highlight the focal point. 

Rules of thumb

Prioritize Functionality

Design With Functionality In Mind. Ensure That Each Piece Of Furniture And Decor Serves A Purpose And Contributes To The Overall Usability Of The Space. This Approach Enhances Both The Aesthetic And Practical Aspects Of Your Design.

Odd Numbers Rule

Grouping Items In Odd Numbers (e.g., Three Or Five) Tends To Be More Visually Interesting And Dynamic Than Even-Numbered Groupings. Use This Rule When Arranging Decor Items Like Vases, Candles, Or Picture Frames.

Contrast Creates Interest

Incorporating Contrasting Elements, Such As Light And Dark Colors, Smooth And Rough Textures, Or Modern And Vintage Pieces, To Add Depth And Interest To Your Design. Contrast Helps To Highlight Different Aspects Of The Room And Keeps The Eye Engaged.

Negative Space

Negative Space, Or The Empty Areas Around Furniture And Decor, Is Crucial For Creating A Balanced And Uncluttered Look. Ensure There Is Enough Negative Space To Allow The Room To Breathe And Avoid Overcrowding.

Visual Hierarchy

Establish A Visual Hierarchy By Arranging Elements In Order Of Importance. Use Size, Color, And Placement To Guide The Viewer's Eye To The Most Significant Parts Of The Room. This Helps In Creating A Structured And Organized Design.

Golden Ratio

(Approximately 1.618) Is A Mathematical Ratio Often Found In Nature And Art. Divide A Line Into Two Sections Where One Part Is About 1.618 Feet And The Other Is About 1 Foot. This Way The Space Will Feel More Balanced And Visually Appealing.
